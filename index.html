What is erectile dysfunction?

Erectile dysfunction (ED), defined as difficulty in achieving or maintaining an erection, is a common experience for many men. In fact, research shows that about 40% of men under 40 have faced it, and nearly 70% by the age of 70.9

Thankfully, ED is an extensively studied condition, and there are numerous effective treatment options available that can help you maintain a longer and stronger erection. Premature ejaculation, another common concern, is often treated separately from ED with targeted medications or techniques that improve control.

Symptoms of erectile dysfunction

At some point in their lives, nearly every man will experience symptoms of ED.5 It is not always a lifelong condition meaning symptoms can fluctuate, appearing occasionally or becoming more persistent over time.

The primary signs of ED include:

  • Trouble getting an erection
  • Difficulty maintaining an erection during sexual activity

Getting an accurate diagnosis often begins by discussing these symptoms with a healthcare provider. In more serious cases, or when prescription medication is not effective, a physical exam or additional tests may be recommended to uncover any underlying conditions contributing to the ED.

Possible causes of erectile dysfunction5

Physical health conditions

  • Aging
  • Glaucoma
  • Blood vessel disorders
  • Blockage of arteries (high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, atherosclerosis)
  • Heart problems
  • Hormonal disorders or changes
  • Injuries
  • Low blood oxygen levels (chronic lung disorders)
  • Nerve disorders (multiple sclerosis, spinal cord injuries)
  • Prostatitis (prostate inflammation)
  • Stroke
  • Surgery
  • Testosterone deficiency
  • Thyroid disorders

Mental health

  • Depression
  • Performance anxiety
  • Stress

Lifestyle

  • Drug use
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Poor diet
  • Smoking

Medications

The Journal of Sexual Medicine identifies several classes of drugs that can contribute to ED, including:

  • Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs): used to treat depression and anxiety
  • Antipsychotics: prescribed for conditions like schizophrenia and bipolar disorder
  • Antihypertensives (older-generation drugs like beta-blockers and diuretics): used to manage high blood pressure

How erectile dysfunction treatments work

Oral ED treatments (pills, tablets, etc.) typically work by blocking the PDE5 enzyme to increase blood flow to the penis. It's important to note that these medications do not create sexual desire or cause a testosterone boost—they simply aid in the physical response needed to get and maintain an erection. Product advertised as the “best sex pills for men” or "male enhancement pills" can be misleading. The only medically recognized treatments are FDA-approved ED medications that enhance blood flow and support normal erectile function.

Pro Tip: Enhancing sexual desire
Most ED medications help with physical performance, but they can’t increase desire—it’s a complex issue that goes beyond pills. For a proven fix, these simple habits can make a real difference and even cause a testosterone boost: eating and sleeping well, staying active, managing stress, connecting emotionally, and trying new things with partner(s).

Is premature ejaculation treatment the same as ED treatment?

Premature ejaculation treatment and ED treatment are not the same, though both address sexual performance issues. Premature ejaculation refers to ejaculating sooner than desired during sex, while ED involves trouble getting or keeping an erection.

A premature ejaculation treatment typically focuses on improving stamina and delaying climax. This can include behavioral strategies, climax control pills, or topical numbing agents. In contrast, ED medications target blood flow.

While some men may deal with both conditions, premature ejaculation pills and treatment plans are tailored differently. Options like premature ejaculation pills may help increase control, but they don’t replace ED medications.

Best erectile dysfunction pills

Popular ED pills include options that enhance blood flow to the penis, providing effective relief for many men experiencing difficulty achieving an erection. There's no such thing as "male enhancement pills" or “sex pills for men,” but there are clinically tested ED medication erection pills. Rugiet, Maximus, and Hims are popular picks for their long-lasting, fast-acting, and combination products.

Common guidelines for ED pills:

  • When to take: 15 to 60 minutes before sex
  • Duration: four to 36 hours
  • How to take: some work best on an empty stomach, others with a meal
  • Effectiveness: FDA-approved ED pills are effective in roughly 74% of men4

Best blood pressure medication for erectile dysfunction

Certain blood pressure medications have been linked to ED, including beta-blockers and diuretics. Alpha-blockers, ACE inhibitors, and ARBs may be less likely to cause ED. Some research suggests the link between blood pressure medication and ED may be more psychological than physical, and that fear of potential side effects—or other unrelated health issues, can trigger ED symptoms.1

Best statin for erectile dysfunction

Statins are a class of drugs primarily prescribed to lower cholesterol, not as a direct treatment for ED. However, if your ED is linked to high cholesterol from conditions like cardiovascular disease, peripheral artery disease, diabetes, or high blood pressure, statins may improve erectile function when used alongside common prescription ED medications.11

Best nitric oxide supplement for erectile dysfunction

Nitric oxide is a vital compound for many bodily functions, including hormone release, neutransmission, and blood vessel dialation, which is crucial for maintaining erections.3,6 Production of this compound is fuelled by amino acids like L-citrulline and L-arganine.

L-citrulline and L-arganine supplements or foods rich in L-citrulline and L-arganine (nuts, meat, watermelon, legumes, dairy products, red meat, fish and poultry) may or may not be effective in boosting sexual performance in men–the science varies.6

Best testosterone boost for erectile dysfunction / Best natural testosterone boost for erectile dysfunction

Testosterone levels naturally decrease as men age, but lifestyle factors may help slow this decline.

A balanced diet rich in leafy greens, fatty fish, healthy oils, and adequate zinc intake can support a testosterone boost in the body. Regular exercise, weight lifting, managing stress, getting enough sleep, and maintaining vitamin D levels are also key.2,7

While some herbal supplements claim to boost testosterone, the science is inconclusive.

Testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) is the only FDA-approved treatment for low testosterone. It must be prescribed by doctors and is administered via injections, gels, patches, implants, or oral methods. (Only 0.9% to 2.9% of U.S. men are prescribed TRT.)10

Best vitamin for erectile dysfunction

Some vitamins and supplements may help alleviate ED symptoms, but they are not as effective as prescription medications.8 Vitamins that support male sexual health can include B3, B9, B12, C, and D.

A balanced diet and sun exposure typically provides sufficient vitamins and taking excessive amounts can be harmful, interact with medications, or cause side effects, unless advised by a doctor.

Best cream for erectile dysfunction

Eroxon, the only FDA-authorized ED gel, is available without a prescription and works by stimulating nerves through cooling and heating effects. Some men report mild skin irritation, discomfort at the application site, painful erections–And, there’s of course the possibility that any negative effects of creams can affect your partner(s) upon physical contact.

Best medicine for erectile dysfunction without side effects

Though the effects are usually mild and temporary, ED meds can cause headaches, flushing, indigestion, nasal congestion, dizziness, and mild vision disturbances.

Side effects vary from person to person, and the best ED medication without side effects will depend on your unique health profile. Low-dose pills can be an effective way to manage symptoms with potentially fewer drawbacks.

Best premature ejaculation treatment

Premature ejaculation (PE) is another common concern that can be addressed with various premature ejaculation treatments, typically climax control pills (prescription premature ejaculation pills designed to help delay climax), behavioral techniques, or topical treatments.

Best vacuum pump for erectile dysfunction

A vacuum pump for erectile dysfunction draws blood into the penis via a hand- or batter-operated pump, and a constriction ring is placed at the base of the penis to maintain the erection. Results are often temporary, requiring consistent and immediate use before sexual activity. The ring should be removed after about 30 minutes to avoid potential side effects.

Vacuum pumps for ED can be purchased online without a prescription, or a doctor can recommend specific models and brands.

Best shockwave therapy machine for erectile dysfunction

Shockwave therapy machines for erectile dysfunction use low-intensity sound waves to stimulate the growth of new blood vessels in the penis. It designed for men with more persistent cases of ED or men who don’t respond to oral ED medication.

The procedure requires specialized equipment and is typically performed in a medical office or clinic. At-home devices are available, but their effectiveness and safety may not be as well-supported.

Best injection for erectile dysfunction

ED injections are self-administered at home with a small needle before each sexual encounter. While they work quickly and reliably, these injections are typically reserved for men who don’t respond to ED erection pills.

Best pills to help erection strength

Several oral medications are formulated as pills to help erection strength by increasing blood flow to the penis. These include well-known prescription options that target the physical mechanisms of ED, helping men achieve a stronger erection during sexual activity.

PDE5 inhibitors like sildenafil, tadalafil, and vardenafil are FDA-approved and widely prescribed erection pills. While these are not aphrodisiacs, they can support longer-lasting, stronger erections in men diagnosed with erectile dysfunction.

Alternative erectile dysfunction treatments

OTC

Despite many sex shops, bodegas and gas stations selling products labeled as the “best sex pill,” there are no FDA-approved over-the-counter (OTC) oral medications for ED. While various supplements are marketed as ‘miracle cures,’ their side effects can range from mild to severe depending on your health and medical history.

Lifestyle changes

Improving lifestyle habits—such as exercising regularly, maintaining a healthy diet, reducing alcohol consumption, and quitting smoking—can potentially improve ED symptoms, result in a strong erection, and have positive effects on sexual performance.4

Can treatment cure erectile dysfunction?

Recent statistics on ED suggest that reversibility is possible for many men. According to newer research, around 32% of men experience remission from ED within five years, particularly with the right treatments.

While this varies depending on the underlying cause, lifestyle improvements, psychological interventions (such as therapy for performance anxiety), and medications often play a significant role in managing or reversing the condition.

Please note: Common side effects of ED medications are typically mild, ranging from back pain to nasal congestion. It’s important to follow a healthcare provider’s guidance when taking new medications or supplements.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How soon should I take ED treatment before having sex?
The timing depends on the medication. Some ED pills need to be taken 30-60 minutes before activity, while others can be taken up to 15 minutes prior. Review the specific product for guidance.
Is it safe to combine ED treatments?
Combining ED treatments is not typically recommended without consulting a healthcare provider. Different treatments may interact and lead to unwanted side effects or complications.
What’s the difference between name-brand and generics?
Generic ED medications offer the same benefits as name-brand versions because they contain the same active ingredients. They are regulated by health authorities, like the FDA, to meet the same standards for safety, quality, and effectiveness. The key difference is that generics are typically more affordable, making them a budget-friendly option.
Is there anything OTC that works for ED?
Over-the-counter options exist, but they are not FDA-approved for treating ED and may not be as effective as prescription medications. It's advised you consult a healthcare provider before trying any OTC products.
Can ED treatment help me regain strength?
ED treatments are designed to improve erectile function by increasing blood flow and addressing the underlying causes of ED. Many men can experience stronger and more consistent erections with proper treatment.
Can I fix ED naturally at home?
Lifestyle changes like regular exercise, maintaining a healthy diet, reducing stress, and quitting smoking may help improve erectile function if one of these reasons contributes to the root cause of your ED.
How do you know whether ED is due to a physical or mental factor?
ED can stem from physical or psychological causes, or a combination of both. Consistent symptoms may suggest a physical cause, while situational ED could point to psychological factors. A healthcare provider can help identify the root cause through evaluation and recommend appropriate treatment.
Are online ED prescriptions covered by insurance?
Some online platforms may accept insurance for ED medications. These sites typically require a copay and/or membership fee. However, many sites do not accept insurance but offer generic versions of ED medications at prices as low as $2 per pill, making them a highly affordable option without coverage.
Does my doctor need to know I'm getting ED treatment online?
It may not be required to inform your general practitioner, but it's highly recommended. Sharing this information helps ensure your overall health is managed properly and avoids potential medication interactions.
Can a sex pill help with psychologically based ED?
Psychological ED doesn’t always respond to a typical sex pill the same way physical ED does. The best sex pill for someone with mental ED might still offer temporary help by improving confidence during sexual activity. Some men find that combining a sex pill with therapy or stress management leads to better results.
Is there such a thing as male enhancement pills?
ED treatments exist, but male enhancement pills for stamina, libido, or size aren’t backed by solid science. Most male enhancement pills are unregulated supplements with mixed results.
